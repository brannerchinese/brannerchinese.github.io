Lǔ Xùn 魯迅 (1881–1936) on the truth behind Confucian morality (1918)

Permalink: 2012-06-09 10:24:00 by David Prager Branner in

Lǔ Xùn 魯迅 has his main character say:

我翻开历史一查，这历史没有年代，歪歪斜斜的每页上都写着「仁义道德」几个字。我横竖睡不着，仔细看了半夜，才从字缝里看出字来，满本都写着两个字是“吃人”！

I flipped open a history book and looked through it. The era of the history was unspecified, but the words "goodness and virtue" were scrawled across every page. I simply couldn't get to sleep, and after reading it closely for most of the night I was finally able to make out characters written invisibly in the spaces between other characters — the whole book said nothing but "oppress and cheat people"! (lit. "eat people")

— "Kuángrén rìjì" 狂人日記 [Diary of a maniac] (1918).
